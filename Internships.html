Stable Hexapod locomotion on Uneven Terrain

The research aims at achieving stable locomotion using torque based Central Pattern Generators for the Daisy Hexapod by Hebi Robotics exposed to constant perturbations on an unknown terrain .

  • Formulated the kinematics and the state-space model for the dynamics of the hexapod.
  • Built a torque controlled CPG for locomotion and an Impedance Controller for increased leg stability on uneven terrain.
  • The simulations are carried out in a PyBullet and Webots simulation environment.

    Autonomous UV-C Sterilization Robot

    The product aims to autonomously sterilize rooms in Hospitals using UV-C rays which is claimed to be more efficient than the conventional sterilisation process.

  • Designed a Power distribution PCB for the UV arm.
  • Programmed and assembled the 2-DoF UV arm and automated its motion using limit switches and ATmega controller.
  • Programmed the mecanum wheel drive for trajectory mapping on Teensy microcontroller.
  • Worked on real time trajectory storage for when the robot is operated in the manual mode.
  • The stored trajectory was then traced back autonomously during the UV sterilization process.
